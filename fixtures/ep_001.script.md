# EPISODE: ep_001 — Last Call at the Edge of Nowhere

This is APPROVED SOURCE MATERIAL. The producer translates each scene; it
transcribes, it does not write. Dialogue must be reproduced verbatim, in
order, attributed to the correct speaker.

## VOCABULARY

- location_tag: `small_bar_interior`
- time_of_day: `night`
- actors: `hero` (role_tag `protagonist`), `bartender` (role_tag `bartender`)
- framings: `establishing`, `close_on` (needs subject_actor_id), `two_shot`

## SCENE: sc_bar_intro_001

### Scene

A small bar interior at night. The hero sits at the counter nursing silence;
the bartender polishes a glass. Three story beats, each covered by one shot.

### Beat 0 — sparse opening exchange (both actors) — shot: establishing

- HERO: "Quiet night."
- BARTENDER: "Always is. Right up until it isn't."

### Beat 1 — bartender reads the hero, pours a drink unasked (bartender only) — shot: close on the bartender

- BARTENDER: "You look like a man who came here to not talk about it."
- BARTENDER: "First one's on the house, then."

### Beat 2 — hero concedes with dry humor; the ice breaks (both actors) — shot: two-shot

- HERO: "That obvious, huh."
- BARTENDER: "Only to bartenders and dogs, friend."

## SCENE: sc_bar_last_call_002

### Scene

Same bar, later the same night. The crowd never came. The bartender starts
closing up while the hero stays planted on his stool. Two beats, two shots.

### Beat 0 — last call lands (both actors) — shot: two-shot

- BARTENDER: "Last call. Not that anyone's rushing you."
- HERO: "One more. For the road that isn't going anywhere."

### Beat 1 — the hero almost says it (hero focus) — shot: close on the hero

- HERO: "You ever feel like the night knows something you don't?"
- HERO: "Forget it. Pour."

## SCENE: sc_rooftop_007

- location: rooftop_garden

The rooftop garden above the bar, night. The hero looks out over the city.
